My Top 3 Ted Talks

Title & Speaker Video Summary
Do It Yourself! | Nardwuar Often times we try to find the "easy way out" in efforts of avoiding any hard work. Nardwuar however, discusses the importance of getting to it and doing things yourself - and how that gurantees even better results than what you expected.
The Standing Rock resistance and our fight for indigenous rights | Tara Houska Tara Houska brings up the often forgotten issues that exists within the Native American population in America. Morover, she brings to light that the actions of the past (Native American genocide) are still affecting the way Natives live. She then mentions how the Natives are resisting and are fighting in hopes of gaining back what they once lost and heal the wounds that were made.
The art of misderection | Apollo Robbins Apollo Robbins preforms the art of pickpottin infront of an entire audience without them noticing a thing. He does this in order to show how there are flaws within our perception that allow things to happen right in front of you, like getting your watch stolen, without you noticing.

My Top 3 RSA Animations

Title & Speaker Video Summary
Superfreakonomics | Steven Levitt & Stpehen Dubner _________________________________ This video goes over the idea of altruism which is a fancy word for the idea of being selfless. It discusses scientific studies in which people were given $10 and then asked if they wanted to share that amount with another person in another room who they would never see. Ultimately, it was found that people usually tended to give away $3. However, when conditions were changed, for example both parties had money and could now steal from the other, then people would not be as inclined to give but rather take. The conclusion was that people in this experiment were not so much selfless, but just wanted to appear as such
The Pradox of Choice | Professor Renata Salecl Renata Salecl brings up an interesting view on the idea of choice and how having choices or the ability to choose, can lead to nothing being done at all. At the heart of it, she explains how our choices are pressured by anxiety and the fact that in making a choice, you will always lose something else. These things then lead to nothing being done within society as, given the ability to make ones own choice, leads to nothing being done at all.
The Empathetic Civilisation | Jeremy Rifkin Jeremy Rifkin explores the idea that humans are an Empathetic society. He explains how this is a fact as our brains & neurons react and imitate the emotions that others go through. He says that it is the empathy that ultimately connects us and we have to start having this same mindset.
